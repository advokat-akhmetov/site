VISA OR FRAUD?

When handing over money and passports for visa processing becomes a matter for pre-trial criminal investigation under Article 190 of the Criminal Code of Kazakhstan.

Language / Язык / 语言:
Русский | English | 中文

If you came to this page from Instagram, the issue probably caught your attention because it often arises in legal practice: foreign citizens hand over money, passports and documents to an intermediary who promises to arrange a visa, a lawful stay permit or other migration-related documents in Kazakhstan.

Time passes. The visas are not issued. The money is not returned. Passports were handed over to the intermediary. The lawful period of stay of the foreign citizens has expired. The key question arises: is this merely a failure to perform obligations, or are there signs of criminal fraud?

In cases like this, a mistake in the first written complaint may determine the entire direction of the investigation.

Why this is not always a civil dispute

In such situations, dishonest intermediaries often try to present the matter as an ordinary dispute between a customer and a service provider: the service allegedly failed, documents were delayed, difficulties arose, and the issue should supposedly be resolved only through a civil court.

However, if money and documents were obtained through deception or abuse of trust, and there was no real intention to perform the promised actions from the beginning, the situation goes beyond ordinary civil-law relations.

In that case, the matter may contain signs of a criminal offence under Article 190 of the Criminal Code of the Republic of Kazakhstan — fraud.

When criminal-law risk arises

The circumstances under which money and documents were transferred are crucial. If a person promised to arrange a C3 visa, extend lawful stay, assist with migration status or carry out other legal actions, but after receiving the money took no real steps, the matter requires criminal-law assessment.

In practice, attention is paid not only to the fact that the visa was not issued. The conduct of the intermediary before receiving the money, the promises made, correspondence, the real ability to provide the service, further actions after receiving the money and the reaction to refund requests are all important.

For a criminal case, it is not enough to show that the visa was not issued. It is necessary to demonstrate the method by which the money was obtained, the nature of the promises, the conduct of the intermediary and signs of deception or abuse of trust.

Why a lawyer is needed at the police complaint stage

Such cases are considered within the framework of a pre-trial investigation by the police or other law enforcement authorities. Therefore, the participation of a lawyer is important from the very beginning.

Many victims make the same mistake: they describe the situation too simply — “they took the money and did not make the visa.” For an investigator, such wording may look like an ordinary failure to perform contractual obligations.

For proper registration and investigation, the full picture must be disclosed: how trust was established, what promises were made, what documents were transferred, what sums were received, what actions were allegedly to be performed and what happened after the money was received.

What should be reflected in the complaint

A legally prepared complaint should not be a collection of emotions. It must show signs of a criminal offence.

In cases of this kind, it is usually important to reflect:

  • who exactly received the money and documents;
  • under what circumstances trust in that person arose;
  • what promises were given to the foreign citizens;
  • what amount was transferred and by what method;
  • whether passports, copies of documents, powers of attorney or other materials were handed over;
  • what actions the intermediary promised to perform;
  • whether the intermediary had a real ability to arrange the visa;
  • what happened after the money was received;
  • whether there were attempts to hide, delay, or shift responsibility;
  • whether there are other victims.

These circumstances allow law enforcement authorities to see not a simple everyday conflict, but a possible scheme of obtaining money under knowingly unfulfilled promises.

What evidence matters

In fraud cases, evidence must be collected quickly. The more time passes, the higher the risk that correspondence will be deleted, messages will disappear, phone numbers will change, bank accounts will be closed and participants will become difficult to locate.

The most important evidence usually includes:

  • correspondence in WhatsApp, Telegram, WeChat and other messengers;
  • bank transfers and receipts;
  • written acknowledgements of receiving money;
  • contracts or other written documents;
  • photos or copies of passports handed over;
  • audio and video recordings of negotiations;
  • information about witnesses to the transfer of money or documents;
  • screenshots of promises, deadlines and guarantees;
  • documents confirming expiry of the lawful stay period of foreign citizens.
In criminal proceedings, the value lies not in one isolated document, but in the totality of evidence. That is why a lawyer evaluates not only the fact that money was transferred, but the entire sequence of events.

Why the initial intent matters

The key issue in such cases is whether the intermediary actually intended to provide the promised service, or whether trust was used from the beginning to obtain money.

If a person received money, promised to arrange a visa, had no real ability to do so, took no necessary steps, misled the victims and avoided refunding the money, these circumstances may be significant for criminal qualification.

At the same time, the conclusion that there are signs of fraud is not based on one fact alone. It is formed from the person’s conduct before, during and after receiving the money.

The role of a lawyer in pre-trial investigation

In such cases, a lawyer’s work is not limited to drafting a complaint. Legal assistance may include representation of victims, preparation of motions, participation in interrogations, appeals against unlawful decisions, control over the completeness of the investigation and formation of the evidentiary position.

At the pre-trial investigation stage, a lawyer may:

  • properly formulate the circumstances of the offence;
  • prepare a criminal complaint;
  • seek registration of the case in the Unified Register of Pre-Trial Investigations;
  • accompany victims during questioning;
  • file motions for investigative actions;
  • seek disclosure of banking information;
  • initiate questioning of witnesses;
  • appeal investigator inaction or unlawful decisions;
  • prevent the matter from being artificially reduced to a civil dispute.

If the victims are foreign citizens

When the victims are foreign citizens, the case becomes even more complicated. They may not know the language, may not understand how to contact law enforcement authorities, may fear consequences related to migration status and may not know how to protect their rights in Kazakhstan.

In such a situation, the lawyer becomes the link between the victims, law enforcement authorities, translators, migration bodies and the court.

It is especially important to prevent victims from giving incomplete or inaccurate explanations due to unfamiliarity with the procedure, as this may later weaken the legal position.

Why waiting is dangerous

Delay is dangerous in such cases. While victims wait for another promise, evidence may disappear, accounts may be closed, phone numbers may change, correspondence may be deleted and the person who received the money may leave the region or the country.

The earlier legal work begins, the greater the opportunity to preserve evidence and direct the investigation correctly.

Practical conclusion

If foreign citizens handed over money and passports under the promise of visa processing and the service was not performed, the situation should not automatically be treated as an ordinary civil dispute.

If there are signs of deception or abuse of trust, the matter should be considered in the criminal-law sphere.

In such cases, the participation of a lawyer is necessary already at the pre-trial investigation stage, because this is when the foundation of the future criminal case is formed.

VISA OR FRAUD? The answer depends not on promises, but on evidence, conduct and correct legal qualification.
